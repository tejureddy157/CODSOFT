Processes

Overview:

A rice mill is an agricultural processing facility where paddy rice is converted into white rice through a series of mechanical and chemical processes.
This transformation involves cleaning, hulling, milling, and polishing the rice grains.
Rice mills vary in size and capacity, ranging from small, family-operated units to large industrial plants.

Key processes:

CLICK HERE TO VIEW COMPLETE PROCESS MAP
  1. Cleaning: Removing impurities like straw, dust, and stones from the paddy.
  2. Husking: Separating the husk (outer shell) from the rice grains.
  3. Milling: Removing the bran layer to produce white rice.
  4. Polishing: Enhancing the appearance by removing any remaining bran particles and polishing the rice.
  5. Grading and Sorting: Categorizing rice based on size, quality, and color.
